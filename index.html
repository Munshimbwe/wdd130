Broken Hill

Kabwe, formerly known as Broken Hill, is the capital of Zambia's central province and a major transport hub located 139 km north of Lusaka. Founded in 1902 after discovering lead and zinc, it is historically significant as the birth place of Zambian politics and site of the of the 1921 "Broken Hill Man" fossil discovery.
